General

5 questions
Q What is Ember & Root?
Ember & Root is a wood-fired pizza restaurant in Karuizawa, Nagano, Japan. We source all ingredients from farms within 120 kilometers of our location. Every pizza is made with 72-hour fermented dough and cooked in our 900°F oven. We partner with 0 family farms across Nagano Prefecture.
Q Why the name "Ember & Root"?
"Ember" represents our wood-fired oven and the fire that transforms simple ingredients into something magical. "Root" represents our connection to the land—the 0 farms we partner with and the Nagano soil that grows our ingredients. Together, they capture our philosophy: fire reveals what the earth provides.
Q When did you open?
Ember & Root opened in November 2019. Founder Kenji Matsumoto spent six months building our oven by hand—900 bricks and countless burns. We started with just two pizzas and have grown to 12, but our commitment to local sourcing has never wavered.
Q Do you speak English?
Yes! Our staff is bilingual in Japanese and English. We welcome international visitors to Karuizawa. Our menu is available in both languages, and we're happy to answer any questions you have.
Q Is Ember & Root family-friendly?
Absolutely! We welcome families with children. We have high chairs available and our pizzas are perfect for sharing. Just be mindful that our oven is very hot (900°F) and we ask that children are supervised at all times.

Ordering & Pickup

4 questions
Q Do you take reservations?
No, we don't take reservations. We're a small space and believe in first-come, first-served. Come early—we sell out most nights by 20:00. For groups larger than 10 people, please contact us at least two weeks in advance.
Q Do you offer delivery?
No, we don't offer delivery. Our pizzas are best enjoyed fresh from the oven—they lose their magic in a delivery bag. We're pickup only from our Karuizawa location, just a 5-minute walk from Karuizawa Station.
Q How long does pickup take?
Please allow 20-30 minutes for order preparation during peak hours. During busy nights (weekends), wait times may be longer.
Q Can I cancel or modify my order?
You can cancel or modify your order before we start preparing it. Once we've started making your pizza (usually within 5-10 minutes of ordering), we cannot make changes or issue refunds. Please double-check your order before submitting!

Food & Ingredients

5 questions
Q Where do your ingredients come from?
Everything comes from within 120 kilometers of Karuizawa. We partner with 0 family farms across Nagano Prefecture—from Asama Farm's tomatoes to Tateshina Mushroom Forest's wild mushrooms to the Nagano Dairy Collective's cheese. We visit every farm each season. See our farm map →
Q What's special about your dough?
Our dough is fermented for 72 hours—three full days. This slow fermentation develops complex flavors and creates a crust that's crispy on the outside, chewy on the inside, and easy to digest. We use flour from Nagano wheat and make fresh dough every morning at 5:30 AM.
Q Why 900°F?
900°F (480°C) is the traditional Neapolitan pizza temperature. At this heat, our pizzas cook in just 90 seconds. The high heat creates the perfect leopard-spotted crust—charred in spots but still tender. We read the flame, not thermometers. The crust tells us when it's ready.
Q Do you have seasonal pizzas?
Yes! Our menu evolves with the harvest. For example, our Harvest Pumpkin pizza is only available October-December when Nagano pumpkins are at their peak. We also have weekly specials based on what farmers tell us is peaking. Follow us on social media for updates!
Q What makes your pizzas different?
Three things: 1) Our ingredients—all sourced within 120km of Karuizawa. 2) Our dough—72-hour fermentation. 3) Our oven—900°F, hand-built, wood-fired. But honestly? The difference is intention. Every ingredient has a story, every pizza has a purpose. We're not just making pizza; we're celebrating Nagano's farms. We currently offer 12 pizzas on our menu.

Allergens & Dietary

4 questions
Q Do you have vegetarian and vegan options?
Yes! Most of our pizzas are vegetarian—look for the vegetarian badge. For vegans, our Marinara pizza (tomato, garlic, oregano, olive oil) is completely plant-based. We can also modify other pizzas to be vegan upon request (please ask when ordering).
Q What allergens are in your kitchen?
Our kitchen handles wheat, dairy (milk, cheese), eggs, tree nuts (walnuts), and soy. While we take precautions, cross-contamination is possible. Please inform us of any allergies when ordering so we can do our best to accommodate you.
Q Can you accommodate other dietary restrictions?
We'll do our best! Please let us know your dietary needs when ordering. We can modify most pizzas (no cheese, extra vegetables, etc.). However, because we're a small kitchen with a specific menu, we may not be able to accommodate all requests. Contact us in advance for special needs.

Business & Partnerships

3 questions
Q Do you offer catering?
Currently, we don't offer catering services. Our pizzas are best enjoyed fresh from our oven, and we're a small operation focused on our Karuizawa location. For special events, please contact us—we may be able to accommodate limited private bookings on select evenings.
